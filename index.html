Who are Cosmic Vanguard?

Cosmic Vanguard is a research group focused on advanced AI methods for modern astronomy. We combine interpretable machine learning, physics-informed models, and robust data-driven workflows to study galaxy evolution, quasar variability, rare transients, and stellar population characterization.

Our Domain

📖 Background

Astronomy has entered a petabyte-scale era where LSST-class pipelines generate massive archives and millions of nightly alerts. Traditional manual and semi-manual workflows are becoming computationally infeasible, requiring interpretable and uncertainty-aware AI systems that can operate reliably at survey scale.

⚠ Research Problem

  • Telescopic limitations hinder detailed morphology analysis.
  • Traditional methods struggle with distant and astrophysically ambiguous galaxies.
  • Rare transient analysis is difficult with sparse labels and class imbalance.
  • Black-box models reduce trust, auditability, and scientific adoption.
  • Observatories need real-time triage under limited follow-up resources.

🔍 Research Gap

Many high-performing models still fail to preserve interpretability and physical consistency. In astronomy, uncertainty should reveal physical degeneracy rather than be treated as mere error. Our approach combines physics-constrained learning with explainable uncertainty outputs usable for decision support.

🎯 Main Objectives

  • Build physics-constrained models for galaxy evolution and robust inference.
  • Calibrate predictive uncertainty as a scientific proxy for astrophysical degeneracy.
  • Model quasar luminosity changes with attention-augmented RNNs.
  • Use contrastive learning for rare transient classification.
  • Infer stellar properties via self-supervised learning.

💼 Operational Impact

  • Survey triage and ranking for large observatory alert streams.
  • Decision-support workflows to optimize spectroscopic follow-up.
  • Scalable catalog intelligence APIs for research and space-tech teams.
  • Trustworthy interpretability modules for real-world scientific AI.

Research Contributions

⚛ Galaxy Evolution (PINN + Explainable Uncertainty)

We integrate physics-informed learning with probabilistic uncertainty modeling to expose where galaxy photometry is astrophysically degenerate. Instead of a single-point prediction, the module produces interpretable uncertainty maps that support follow-up prioritization and scientifically grounded decision-making.

📈 Quasar Variability

We model irregular quasar light curves using attention-aware temporal learning, enabling robust non-linear forecasting under sparse and uneven observation intervals. This improves both predictive stability and interpretability for observatory scheduling workflows.

☄ Transient Classification

We apply self-supervised contrastive learning to improve rare-event separation in highly imbalanced datasets. The resulting representation quality supports faster and more reliable transient triage for large alert streams.

⭐ Stellar Population Modeling

We use disentangled self-supervised representation learning to estimate stellar properties such as mass and metallicity while reducing redshift-related confounding. This enables scalable analysis across large unlabeled survey catalogs.
